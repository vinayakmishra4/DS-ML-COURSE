Independent and Dependent Events 


Q1. Coin + Die (Independent events)

* Coin → P(Head) = 1/2
* Die → P(4) = 1/6
* Independent ⇒ multiply
    👉 1/2 × 1/6 = 1/12

✔️ Nothing changes between events.

⸻

Q2. Two Kings (with replacement)

* Total cards = 52, Kings = 4
* First draw: P(King) = 4/52
* Card is replaced, so second draw is same
* Second draw: P(King) = 4/52

👉 Multiply:
\frac{4}{52} \times \frac{4}{52} = \frac{16}{2704} = \frac{1}{169}

✔️ Replacement ⇒ independent

⸻

Q3. Two Red Balls (without replacement)

* Total = 8 balls (5 red, 3 blue)

Step 1: First red
P = 5/8

Step 2: One red is gone → now 4 red out of 7
P = 4/7

👉 Multiply:
\frac{5}{8} \times \frac{4}{7} = \frac{20}{56} = \frac{5}{14}

✔️ No replacement ⇒ dependent (numbers change)

⸻

Q4. Two Girls (without replacement)

* Total students = 10 (4 girls, 6 boys)

Step 1: First girl
P = 4/10

Step 2: One girl removed → 3 girls out of 9
P = 3/9

👉 Multiply:
\frac{4}{10} \times \frac{3}{9} = \frac{12}{90} = \frac{2}{15}

✔️ Again dependent

⸻

Q5. Green then White (without replacement)

* Total = 10 balls (3 green, 2 yellow, 5 white)

Step 1: Green
P = 3/10

Step 2: One ball removed → total = 9, white still 5
P = 5/9

👉 Multiply:
\frac{3}{10} \times \frac{5}{9} = \frac{15}{90} = \frac{1}{6}

⸻
