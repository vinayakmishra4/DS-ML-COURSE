Question on the Bayes therom

Question 1:
* 1% of people have a disease → P(A) = 0.01
* The test correctly detects the disease 99% of the time → P(B|A) = 0.99
* The test falsely shows positive in 5% of healthy people → P(B|not A) = 0.05

Step 1: Find total probability of testing positive

A positive result can happen in two ways:

1. Person has the disease and tests positive
2. Person is healthy but still tests positive

P(B) = (0.99 × 0.01) + (0.05 × 0.99)
P(B) = 0.0099 + 0.0495 = 0.0594

Step 2: Apply Bayes’ Theorem

P(A|B) = (0.99 × 0.01) / 0.0594
P(A|B) = 0.0099 / 0.0594 ≈ 0.1667

Final Result:
Even after testing positive, the probability of actually having the disease is about 16.7%.
