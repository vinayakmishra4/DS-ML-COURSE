**Question 1:**
A bag has 3 red, 4 blue, and 5 green balls. One ball is drawn.
Find the probability that the ball is red given that it is not green.

**Answer:**
Not green = red + blue = 3 + 4 = 7
So, P = 3/7

**Question 2:**
A die is rolled.
A: number is prime
B: number is odd

**Answer:**
Prime = {2, 3, 5}
Odd = {1, 3, 5}
Common = {3, 5} → 2 outcomes
Total in B = 3
So, P = 2/3

---

**Question 3:**
A card is drawn from a deck.
A: king
B: red card

**Answer:**
Red cards = 26
Red kings = 2
So, P = 2/26 = 1/13

---

**Question 4:**
A box contains 2 white and 3 black balls. Two balls are drawn without replacement.
Find probability that second is white given first is black.

**Answer:**
After first black: white = 2, total = 4
So, P = 2/4 = 1/2

---

**Question 5:**
P(A) = 0.7, P(B) = 0.5, P(A ∩ B) = 0.4
Find P(A | B)

**Answer:**
P = 0.4 / 0.5 = 0.8
